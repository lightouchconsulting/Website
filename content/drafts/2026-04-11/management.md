# Effective Management in the AI-First Era: A New Paradigm for CIOs
As organisations continue to navigate the complexities of the AI-first era, effective management has become a critical component of success. The rapid evolution of technology has led to a significant shift in the way businesses operate, and CIOs must recognise the need to adapt their management strategies to stay ahead of the curve. In this article, we will explore the key elements of effective management in the AI-first era and provide practical guidance for CIOs looking to drive innovation and competitiveness.

## Rethinking Workforce Development
The AI-first era requires a fundamentally different approach to workforce development. Traditional methods of hiring and training are no longer sufficient, and CIOs must be willing to rethink their approach to building internal capabilities. This includes identifying the key skills required to drive innovation and competitiveness, such as data governance, DevOps, and AI development. By prioritising skills readiness, organisations can bridge the gap between their current capabilities and the needs of the AI-first era. Furthermore, CIOs must also consider the role of vendors and partners in supporting their workforce development strategies, and negotiate effective partnerships to drive innovation and speed.

## The Critical Role of Data Governance
Data governance is a critical component of effective management in the AI-first era. As AI systems become increasingly ubiquitous, the need for robust data governance frameworks has never been more pressing. CIOs must recognise the importance of data governance in driving AI success and take proactive steps to establish effective frameworks. This includes establishing clear policies and procedures for data management, ensuring data quality and integrity, and providing training and support for employees. By prioritising data governance, organisations can unlock the full potential of AI and drive business value.

## Bridging the ROI Gap
Despite the potential benefits of AI, many organisations are struggling to demonstrate a clear return on investment (ROI). This is often due to a lack of effective management and governance frameworks, which can lead to ineffective deployment of AI solutions. CIOs must take a proactive approach to addressing the ROI gap, by establishing clear metrics and benchmarks for AI success. This includes identifying key performance indicators (KPIs) such as revenue growth, cost savings, and customer satisfaction, and tracking progress against these metrics. By taking a data-driven approach to AI deployment, organisations can demonstrate the value of AI and drive business success.

## Your Next Step
As a CIO, your next step is to conduct a thorough review of your organisation's management strategies and identify areas for improvement. This includes assessing your workforce development programmes, data governance frameworks, and AI deployment strategies. Consider the following questions: Are our workforce development programmes aligned with the needs of the AI-first era? Do we have effective data governance frameworks in place to support AI success? Are we demonstrating a clear ROI from our AI investments? By taking a proactive and strategic approach to management, you can drive innovation and competitiveness in your organisation and unlock the full potential of the AI-first era.
