# Navigating the Uncharted Risks of Agentic AI in the Enterprise
The rapid adoption of agentic AI across organisations is introducing a new wave of risks that challenge traditional cybersecurity strategies. As AI systems become more autonomous, the potential for unforeseen vulnerabilities and threats increases, making it essential for CIOs to reassess their risk management approaches. The integration of agentic AI into existing systems can compromise the security of even the most well-defended organisations, as the complexity and unpredictability of these systems create new attack surfaces.

## The Complexity of Agentic AI Risks
The rise of agentic AI is exposing organisations to a broad range of risks, from the intentional or unintentional actions of autonomous systems to the potential for AI-generated code to introduce vulnerabilities into existing codebases. Furthermore, the increasing reliance on open-source software (OSS) and the proliferation of AI-generated content can lead to a phenomenon known as "AI slop," where low-quality or malicious code is introduced into OSS, putting entire ecosystems at risk. CIOs must recognise that these risks are not merely theoretical; they have the potential to compromise the very foundations of their organisations' digital infrastructure.

## Mitigating Agentic AI Risks
To effectively mitigate these risks, organisations must adopt a multi-faceted approach that encompasses both technological and organisational measures. This includes implementing robust testing and validation procedures for AI-generated code, as well as establishing clear guidelines and standards for the development and deployment of agentic AI systems. Moreover, CIOs should invest in employee education and training programmes that focus on the safe and responsible use of AI, ensuring that all stakeholders understand the potential risks and benefits associated with these technologies. By fostering a culture of awareness and vigilance, organisations can reduce the likelihood of AI-related incidents and improve their overall resilience in the face of emerging threats.

## Your Next Step
As you navigate the complex landscape of agentic AI risks, your next step should be to conduct a thorough risk assessment of your organisation's current AI deployments and future plans. This involves identifying potential vulnerabilities, evaluating the effectiveness of existing security measures, and developing a comprehensive strategy for mitigating the risks associated with autonomous systems. By taking a proactive and informed approach to risk management, you can ensure that your organisation is well-equipped to harness the benefits of agentic AI while minimising its potential drawbacks. Begin by assembling a cross-functional team to review your AI programme and identify areas where additional safeguards or controls may be necessary, and then develop a roadmap for implementing these measures to safeguard your organisation's digital future.
