# Architecting for AI-Driven Organisations: The Imperative of Modern Core Technologies
As organisations continue to embark on their digital transformation journeys, the importance of modernising core technologies has become increasingly evident. The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) has been a key driver of this transformation, enabling organisations to unlock new insights, improve operational efficiency, and enhance customer experiences. However, the successful deployment of AI-driven solutions is heavily reliant on the underlying architecture of an organisation's core technologies.

## The Challenges of Legacy Systems
Many organisations are still grappling with legacy systems, including mainframes, that were designed decades ago. These systems were not built to support the scale, complexity, and speed required by modern AI-driven applications. As a result, organisations are often forced to work around these limitations, leading to inefficient workflows, increased costs, and reduced agility. Furthermore, the lack of contextual understanding and transparency in AI decision-making processes can lead to mistrust and skepticism among stakeholders. To overcome these challenges, organisations must recognise the need to modernise their core technologies and develop an architecture that is specifically designed to support AI-driven applications.

## The Emergence of New Architectural Layers
The recent emergence of new architectural layers, such as Agentforce Operations and OpenSearch, is a testament to the growing recognition of the need for modern core technologies. These solutions are designed to provide a foundation for AI-driven applications, enabling organisations to streamline workflows, improve data management, and enhance decision-making processes. Additionally, the development of knowledge graphs, such as Elemental, is helping to address the issue of contextual understanding and transparency in AI decision-making. By providing a unified view of an organisation's data assets and relationships, knowledge graphs can help to build trust and confidence in AI-driven applications.

## Your Next Step
As a CIO, it is essential to take a proactive approach to modernising your organisation's core technologies and developing an architecture that is specifically designed to support AI-driven applications. This requires a thorough assessment of your current technology landscape, identification of areas for improvement, and development of a roadmap for modernisation. Key considerations should include the integration of new architectural layers, the development of knowledge graphs, and the implementation of agile and flexible workflows. By taking these steps, you can ensure that your organisation is well-positioned to unlock the full potential of AI-driven applications and achieve its strategic objectives. Start by convening a cross-functional team to explore the opportunities and challenges of modernising your core technologies and developing an AI-driven architecture – the future of your organisation depends on it.
